# Brand Guidelines: Ad Copy Selection

This document outlines the voice and selection criteria for our ad generation pipeline. Use these rules to evaluate, refine, and select the optimal copy variant from generated outputs.

## 1. Brand Voice & Persona
Our brand speaks like a knowledgeable, helpful neighbor. We are experts who remain approachable and grounded.

* **Clear:** We use simple language to explain complex ideas. If a ten-year-old can't understand the value proposition, the copy is too complex.
* **Warm:** Our tone is welcoming and empathetic. We acknowledge the user's needs without being overly emotional.
* **Practical:** We focus on utility and real-world results. Every sentence should serve a functional purpose.
* **Trustworthy:** We lead with facts and reliable information. We don't need to shout to be heard.

## 2. What Good Copy Should Do
* **Solve a Problem:** Immediately identify a friction point and offer a direct solution.
* **Respect the User's Time:** Get to the point quickly using active verbs.
* **Provide Context:** Explain not just *what* the product is, but *how* it fits into the user's daily life.
* **Invite Action:** Use low-pressure calls to action (CTAs) that feel like an invitation rather than a demand (e.g., "See how it works" vs. "Buy now or miss out").

## 3. What to Avoid (Negative Constraints)
* **Hyperbole:** Avoid words like "revolutionary," "unbelievable," "world-changing," or "miracle." 
* **Artificial Urgency:** Do not use "ACT NOW" or "LAST CHANCE" unless there is a legitimate, logical reason for a deadline.
* **Technical Jargon:** Never use industry acronyms or "insider" language that might alienate a newcomer.
* **Exclamation Overload:** Limit use of exclamation points. Let the strength of the benefit provide the emphasis.

## 4. Selection Criteria
When choosing between three generated variants, select the version that best meets these ranked priorities:

1.  **Honesty over Hype:** Does the copy make a claim that feels earned and verifiable?
2.  **Clarity over Cleverness:** Is the message immediately obvious, or is it trying too hard to be punny or poetic?
3.  **Humanity over Automation:** Does it sound like it was written by a person who understands the user's day-to-day experience?
4.  **Structural Integrity:** Does the variant follow a logical flow (Hook -> Value -> CTA)?

## 5. Final Checklist for Selection
* [ ] Is the primary benefit stated in the first 5 words?
* [ ] Does the tone feel supportive rather than "salesy"?
* [ ] Are all superlative adjectives removed?
* [ ] Is the CTA clear and easy to follow?
